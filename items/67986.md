## Summary

Keep open. This is a recent bundled gateway/WhatsApp hang report with concrete logs and follow-up comments on v2026.4.22. Current main has generic channel health hardening and a WhatsApp watchdog, but I did not find high-confidence source, test, or release evidence that the reported 9-minute mid-session wedge or config-read shutdown symptom is fixed.

## Best Possible Solution

Keep this issue open and investigate it as a bundled gateway/WhatsApp reliability bug. The next maintainer step should be to reproduce on current main with `openclaw health --verbose`, a diagnostics export or stability bundle, and logs around `config.get`; then either publish WhatsApp provider-proven `lastTransportActivityAt` into the channel runtime snapshot and align watchdog/health thresholds, or fix the config-read shutdown path if that trigger is confirmed.

## Evidence

- **Recent actionable report:** The provided discussion reports v2026.4.15 on Windows with webchat and WhatsApp silent for 9 minutes until a WhatsApp 408 triggered health-monitor restart, plus follow-up comments on v2026.4.22 Ubuntu where WebUI requests hit `Disconnected: gateway stopping` and the observed trigger was asking OpenClaw to read configuration. The author is not a maintainer and the labels are `bug` and `bug:crash`, not protected labels requiring automatic keep-open for maintainer ownership.
- **Generic stale-socket recovery requires transport liveness:** Current health evaluation only checks stale sockets when the snapshot is connected and has a numeric `lastTransportActivityAt`; connected channels without transport tracking are considered healthy. That means the generic health monitor is not, by itself, proof that a WhatsApp silent socket will be restarted before WhatsApp emits its own 408.
